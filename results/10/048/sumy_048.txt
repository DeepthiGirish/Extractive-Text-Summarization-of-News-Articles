Sloppy wording of the draft animal welfare bill could leave anglers facing court even though it was not intended, the environment select committee said.Committee chairman Michael Jack said: "Any change in the law as it affects animals always arouses powerful emotions and great public interest.But Mr Jack said the bill "very much had the feel of a 'work in progress', and urged the government to guarantee that "an obligation to consult will be enshrined in law" before it extended legislative plans to areas such as pet fairs, circuses and game bird farms.

Labour and the Conservatives are still telephoning the millions of people who have signed up to make sure they do not get marketing "cold calls". Telephone call centres are expected to be used as never before by all the three major parties in the run-up to the general election. Mr Taylor argues there should be new guidelines so all parties can act in the same way if the watchdog believes the rules allow parties to ring TPS numbers about voting intentions and later urge those people to vote for them.

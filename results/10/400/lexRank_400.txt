Prime Minister Tony Blair called him a "giant" of the Labour movement.
Elected for a Cardiff constituency at the 1945 general election, he represented Cardiff seats for more than 40 years.
As foreign secretary in the early 1970s, Lord Callaghan kept an open mind about the UK's entry into the Common Market, seeing the advantages of the UK's entry.
Conservative peer Lord Heseltine said that despite their political differences, he and Lord Callaghan became friends.

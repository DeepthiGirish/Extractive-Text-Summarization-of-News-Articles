The Court of Appeal is expected to rule early next week on whether the alliance's challenge has succeeded.
Pro-hunt campaigners claims the 1949 Parliament Act - which extends the right of the House of Commons to overrule the House of Lords - was itself invalid because it was never passed by peers.
Richard Dodd, regional director of the Countryside Alliance, said he expected between 2,000 and 4,000 supporters in Tyneside to make their protest, with hunt horns and placards.
The Countryside Alliance has lodged papers seeking a judicial review on human rights grounds.

MP's shock at long lost daughter  Labour MP Stephen Pound has told of his shock and his joy on learning he fathered a daughter when he was "out of control" during the "wild" 1960s.
That happened when Lucy wrote to his Parliamentary office saying she believed he was her father.
Mr Pound, who is now married with two other teenage children, said: "I was a nutter and I was a sailor but I wasn't dead."
He said his first reaction was to wonder if he was victim of a "set-up" but he then realised all the dates fitted.

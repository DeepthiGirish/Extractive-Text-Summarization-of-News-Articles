Charity boss Gordon Lishman said if a "decisive blow" was struck at the election it would be by older voters who could be relied on to turn out. Age Concern says the over-55s are "united around" key areas of policy they want the government to focus on. The report was welcomed by Conservative shadow pensions secretary David Willetts. "Labour's continued refusal to admit the true extent of the pensions crisis will be one of the monumental failures of this government. " He pointed to Tory plans to increase the basic state pension to reduce means testing, strengthen company pensions and encourage savings. He highlighted the party's promises to raise the basic state pension, provide free long-term care for the elderly and replace council tax, seen as a particular problem for pensioners on fixed incomes.
